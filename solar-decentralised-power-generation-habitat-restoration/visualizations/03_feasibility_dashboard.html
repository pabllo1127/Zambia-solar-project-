Zambia Solar — Feasibility and Economic Impact

Evidence base for distributed solar development in Zambia (2024–2025 data)

Active market — 225 MW+ commissioned by April 2026
AfDB approved $14.54M for the 20 MW Garneton North plant (2025). 33 MWp Kitwe plant operational (2023). 100 MW Chisamba commissioned June 2025. This is not hypothetical.
Proven PPA tariffs — GET FiT achieved 3.999 USc/kWh
First sub-4c/kWh bid in sub-Saharan Africa outside South Africa. Direct industrial PPAs (mining sector) target 7–9 USc/kWh — substantial developer margin.
Government target: 10,000 MW by 2031
Presidential 1,000 MW Solar Initiative actively commissioning. April 2025: project approvals cut from 6 months to 48 hours.
2024 Open Access Regulations — market fully liberalised
IPPs can sign direct PPAs with industrial customers and wheel power through ZESCO without ZESCO as buyer. Structural enabler that did not exist before 2024.
DFI financing available — AfDB, IFC, Proparco, FMO active
Concessional capital at 5–9% vs 12–15% commercial rates. ZESCO JV equity de-risks projects for lenders dramatically.
Currency risk — ZMW volatile historically
Kwacha appreciated 18.2% in 2025 and 12.9% through March 2026. USD-denominated PPAs (standard for mining sector) mitigate this risk.
ZESCO interconnection — 6–18 month lead time
Improving under new regulations but still requires planning. ZESCO JV partnership dramatically accelerates this process.
ZESCO direct off-taker credit risk
For projects selling to ZESCO: balance sheet and subsidy dependency create risk. Mitigated by direct industrial PPAs (mines paying USD tariffs) or ZESCO JV structure.
