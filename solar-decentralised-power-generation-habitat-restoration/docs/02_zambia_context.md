# 02 — Zambia Country Context

## Energy profile

Zambia is a landlocked country of ~19 million people in south-central Africa. Its electricity
sector has historically been dominated by hydroelectric installations on the Kafue and Zambezi
river systems. As of 2024, total installed capacity sits at approximately 3,985 MW, of which
over 80% is hydroelectric.

### The hydropower vulnerability

The 2023–2024 El Niño drought reduced Kariba Dam — the world's largest reservoir by volume —
to an operational output of just **215 MW** against a nameplate capacity of **1,080 MW**.

Consequences:
- ZESCO imported 250 MW from South Africa's Eskom (~10% of normal demand)
- Residential load-shedding reached **12 hours/day** at peak
- Mines asked to curtail power use by **40%**
- GDP growth revised from 2.3% → 1.2% (IMF 2024)
- Agriculture contracted **20.6%** year-on-year
- Estimated economic cost: **USD 1.3 billion (~5% of GDP)**

### Solar capacity (2024)

Solar installed capacity stood at approximately **124 MW** (5.5% of the generation mix).
The pipeline is projected to grow tenfold by 2028 under the government's Presidential
1,000 MW Solar Initiative and Mission 300 programme.

## Industrial demand

The mining sector is the dominant power consumer. The Copperbelt — centred on Kitwe,
Ndola, Mufulira, Chingola, and Solwezi — hosts major copper and cobalt operations:

- First Quantum Minerals (>50% of Zambia's copper output)
- Glencore (Mopani Copper Mines)
- Barrick Gold (Lumwana)

Mining tariff (2024): **USD 0.093/kWh**
LCOE for new solar in Zambia: **USD 0.038–0.055/kWh**
→ Margin for solar IPP at direct industrial PPA: substantial

## Policy and regulatory framework

### Electricity (Open Access) Regulations 2024
The most significant structural reform in decades:
- Allows large consumers to source power directly from any generator via ZESCO wheeling
- Permits power traders to operate openly
- Removes ZESCO's single-buyer monopoly
- Enables IPPs to sign direct PPAs with industrial off-takers
- Introduced Net Metering Regulations 2024

### Presidential 1,000 MW Solar Initiative
Fast-tracks solar project approvals — reduced from 6 months to **48 hours** (April 2025).

### GET FiT Zambia
KfW-backed competitive tender. Lowest successful bid: **3.999 USc/kWh** — first
sub-4 cent tariff achieved in sub-Saharan Africa outside South Africa.

### Mission 300 / National Energy Compact
Zambia launched its National Energy Compact in January 2025, targeting 300 million
Africans with electricity by 2030. Attracts AfDB financing and accelerated government engagement.

## ZESCO investment plan

ZESCO has planned to invest **USD 6.4 billion** in project development from 2023 to 2031,
financed through debt and equity complemented by Special Purpose Vehicles and the
Public Private Partnership model.
