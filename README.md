## Abstract

Zambia faces a structural energy crisis driven by an over-reliance on hydroelectric power, which accounts
for over 80% of installed generation capacity. The 2023–2024 El Niño drought reduced Kariba Dam output
from a nameplate capacity of 1,080 MW to just 215 MW, forcing load-shedding of up to 12 hours per day
and a 40% power curtailment request to the mining sector. The estimated economic cost was USD 1.3 billion,
approximately 5% of GDP.

This research develops a framework for decentralised solar PV generation as a structural complement to
Zambia's vulnerable hydroelectric system. The framework is built on five design principles: small-to-medium
plant scale (1–50 MW) targeting industrial off-takers; underground XLPE cable transmission to eliminate
habitat disruption; integration into existing ZESCO substations to minimise capital expenditure; an
agri-ecological co-location system deploying panel-wash irrigation, goat grazing, and apiculture; and
a Public-Private Partnership joint venture structure with ZESCO as equity co-investor under a 20–25 year
take-or-pay Power Purchase Agreement.

## Research areas

### 1. Solar PV technology
Analysis of commercial monocrystalline silicon PV technology (PERC, bifacial, TOPCon, HJT), achieving
20–26% module efficiency at installed costs of USD 640–980/kWp. Zambia's exceptional solar resource
(5.5–5.78 kWh/m²/day average irradiance, 1,900–2,100 kWh/m² annual GHI) enables specific yields of
1,550–1,700 kWh/kWp/year with a performance ratio of 0.82.

### 2. Generation economics
Levelised Cost of Electricity (LCOE) modelled at USD 0.038–0.055/kWh over a 25-year project life —
well below Zambia's current mining sector tariff of USD 0.093/kWh, establishing a strong commercial
case for direct industrial Power Purchase Agreements. Project IRR modelled at 12–18% (equity).

### 3. Transmission infrastructure
Trade-off analysis between overhead transmission lines (OHL) and underground cable (UGC) at 33 kV.
Underground XLPE armoured cable is recommended for all connections under 8–10 km, with the capital
cost premium (4–10× OHL) representing 3–9% of total project CAPEX — justified by zero habitat
fragmentation, full agrivoltaic compatibility, and superior storm resilience.

### 4. Grid integration
Assessment of Zambia's 2024 Electricity (Open Access) Regulations as the enabling framework for
IPP-to-industrial PPA structures. Integration into existing ZESCO 33/66 kV substations eliminates
USD 500,000–1,500,000 in new substation construction costs and 12–24 months from permitting timelines.

### 5. Agri-ecological co-location
Application of agrivoltaics research to the Zambian context: panel-wash water recycled as drip
irrigation; goat grazing eliminating mechanical mowing costs (USD 10,000–30,000/year on a 10 MW plant);
apiculture generating USD 12,500–60,000/year in honey revenue with documented 412% yield improvement
at agrivoltaic sites (Iowa State University); and native wildflower planting establishing biodiversity
corridors aligned with miombo woodland ecotypes.

### 6. Government joint venture structure
Analysis of the JIGSCO Energy Corporation Limited precedent — a Special Purpose Vehicle with
shareholding between Jigsaw Investments Limited and ZESCO Limited, currently constructing the
100 MW Chirundu Solar Plant (USD 90M, December 2025). This Public-Private Partnership model,
enabled by ZESCO's USD 6.4 billion 2023–2031 investment plan, provides the structural framework
for private developer equity participation alongside government co-investment.

## ZESCO joint venture model

Based on the **JIGSCO Energy Corporation Limited** precedent (Jigsaw Investments + ZESCO,
100 MW Chirundu Solar Plant, USD 90M, groundbreaking December 2025):

```
SPV — jointly owned project company
├── Private developer / operator   →  51–70% equity
├── ZESCO Limited                  →  30–49% equity
└── Community trust (optional)     →   5–10% equity

Revenue flow:
PPA income → debt service (8–10 yrs) → O&M costs → management fees → equity dividends
```

ZESCO sites identified for future JV development:
**Siavonga · Chipata · Mumbwa · Kasama · Kafue Gorge Lower**
